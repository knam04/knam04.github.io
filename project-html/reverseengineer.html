Reverse Engineering as a Component of Digital Ethnography

Ethnographic studies of online labor platforms rarely involve analysis of proprietary platform code. This poster proposes reverse engineering of mobile apps as a way to circumvent access barriers and provide digital ethnographers additional material to study. We applied this reverse engineering method to the home care platform Honor to study how the platform organizes labor and disciplines workers. Simultaneously, we conducted a discourse analysis of digital documents (webpages, blog posts, and videos) published by Honor. The combination of these methods produced greater insights than either method on its own. We found that workers’ autonomy to set preferences and control their schedules is limited by both algorithmic opacity and social norms, and that gamification of labor is framed as “career growth.” The findings in this poster demonstrate how studying reverse-engineered code alongside other, traditional sources of ethnographic data can strengthen claims about platformic management when studying online labor platforms.
