About Me

Hi! My name is Kaz Nam and I am a senior at the Rice University, studying computer science and sociology. I'm originally from New Jersey.

My main research interests are in human-computer interaction (HCI) and information science. I'm interested in how platform design affects user behavior. I'm also interested in how a user's perception of an algorithm affects how they interact with it. I've worked on multiple projects touching on these interests that use both quantitative and qualitative methods at various institutions. So far, I've conducted research with professors at Lehigh University, Rice University, CUNY Hunter, and (most recently) University of Washington. I've also presented at CHI and HRI!

On campus, I'm involved in design and leadership clubs. I'm currently a studio lead for Rice Design for America, where I work to connect local organizations with teams of students interested in learning and applying human-centered design. I have also served as the Community Facilitator Co-coordinator at Rice, a group that serves as liasons between the Office of DEI and the student body. Non-research interests include reading, cafe hopping, and jewlery making.
